About

Our Mission

UTHKPASS is a student-run, non-profit student society at the University of Toronto. Founded by a group of undergraduate students who have a sense of enthusiasm towards Hong Kong affairs, UTHKPASS has strived to promote interest in the social, political and economic affairs of Hong Kong. As the first student society that puts an emphasis on Hong Kong affairs among North American universities/colleges, we aim to raise awareness on public issues happening around the city through our research initiatives and encourage social engagement of Hong Kong students studying at the University of Toronto.

Through organizing different social events and providing connections with several governmental and non-governmental organizations, we aim to offer all members and Hong Kong students at the University of Toronto a broader spectrum of community outreach. On the research aspect, we are going to publish research projects on a monthly basis. Thematic focus will be closely related to latest Hong Kong issues. We kindly look forward to seeing you in our upcoming events!

President - Magi Jury Leung

Magi Jury Leung is a third-year student double majoring in International Relations and Health Studies, with a minor in Contemporary Asian Studies. As the President and the Co-founder of HKPASS, he is responsible to oversee the operation of the club and representing the organization at various events. Beyond HKPASS, Magi is currently the Secretary of the Trinity College Meeting, a member of various governing committees, the Vice-President of U of T Aviation Club, Compliance Analyst for the G20 Research Group. He was also the co-founder of U of T Student Health Association and the council-manager for U of T International Students Association. Last year, He was awarded the Japanese Government Scholarship and attended the University of Tokyo as a special auditor.

Vice President (Media Production) - Michael Linennen

Michael Linennen is a third-year student majoring in Journalism and Media Studies. He worked as a photographer for different organizations and has interviewed a number of celebrities. He has won awards from the National Geographic for two consecutive years. Having been brought up in a diverse family background, it gave him the advantage to explore and evaluate Hong Kong affairs in a global context. Outside of UTHKPASS, Michael is a journalist, photographer, Internal Executive of UTAC, and the Technology Director for U of T Novelty Student Association.

Vice President (Research) - Arthur Lui

Arthur Lui is a third year student majoring in Peace, Conflict and Justice Studies and Contemporary Asian Studies, with a minor in Political Science. Having found that there are limited channels for overseas students from Hong Kong to have a thorough understanding over current issues and the roles of Hong Kong in the global context, Arthur aspires to make a difference by publishing monthly/bi-monthly research projects with the HKPASS Research Team.
